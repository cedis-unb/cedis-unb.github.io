Brasília, October 28, 2024

Labor law is the field in which ordinary citizens most often need to navigate without professional help — and the field in which the language helps them the least. **This imbalance is what the undergraduate thesis defended in October 2024 by João Pedro Alves Machado and Júlio César Martins França addresses**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/andre_lanna" "André Luiz Peron Martins Lanna" >}}.

The duo builds a Progressive Web App (PWA) — behaving like a native app while running through the browser — to bring interactive legal flowcharts to users’ phones. Organized as chained questions, the flows guide citizens through labor-law logic without demanding prior fluency in jargon. The PWA choice is both technical and political: no app store installation, offline support, low storage footprint — three attributes that matter when the target audience is diverse and often underserved by bandwidth.

By pairing {{< link-interno "/areas/software_architecture/" "software architecture" >}} with {{< link-interno "/areas/transformation/" "digital transformation" >}} of access to justice, the thesis extends a project also targeted by another 2024 CEDIS work (by Guilherme Vial and Lucas Xavier) — evidence of a research line that spans duos and reinforces one another.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/42301).
