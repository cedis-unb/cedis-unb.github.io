Brasília, September 11, 2024

Workers who want to understand their rights on vacation, termination, or benefits rarely find a clear answer on the first query — and almost never in a single window. **This is the friction the undergraduate thesis defended in September 2024 by Álvaro Henrique de Sousa Gouvea and Luís Guilherme Gaboardi Lins responds to with a multimodal chatbot**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/sergio_freitas" "Sergio Antônio Andrade de Freitas" >}}.

The work uses Large Language Models as the conversational core and adds multimodal layers — text, image, PDF — to handle the diversity of artifacts the questioner brings to the table: employment contract, receipt, document photo, WhatsApp message. In an {{< link-interno "/areas/ai_pln/" "AI and NLP" >}} setting, the proposal tackles a notoriously tricky slice: legal accuracy must coexist with colloquial language, and the model must know when to declare its limits.

The thesis grounds that conversation in a concrete artifact: a prototype that can be tried, critiqued, and improved. It is a typical contribution to a research line CEDIS has been consolidating since 2023, connecting LLMs to domains of significant public impact.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/40493).
