Brasília, July 17, 2025

UnB’s Gama Campus gathers engineering programs and a student community whose routine demands double attention — academic performance and emotional care. **The undergraduate thesis defended in July 2025 by Delziron Braz de Lima and Fellipe Pereira da Costa Silva proposes a specific piece of that care: an app supporting students’ mental health and well-being**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/george_marsicano" "George Marsicano Corrêa" >}}.

The work departs from a diagnosis of the university environment itself: emotional difficulties coexist with fragile interpersonal relationships, performance strain, and distance from available support services. The duo designs a solution that does not replace professional care — an unrealistic aim — but occupies the middle band between problem awareness and appropriate referral: organized information, self-knowledge tools, risk indicators, and clear paths to seek help.

By tying {{< link-interno "/areas/active_learning/" "active learning" >}} with concrete HCI concerns aimed at a sensitive audience, the thesis delivers a useful product to the local academic community — and an illustrated case for other universities to think through their own arrangements.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/42493).
