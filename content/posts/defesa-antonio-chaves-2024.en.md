Brasília, August 8, 2024

Moodle is the LMS present in most Brazilian universities — and it is precisely where gamification meets its most concrete barrier. Applying game mechanics to a course is not a task for an unsupported instructor. **The undergraduate thesis defended in August 2024 by Antonio Rangel Chaves and Vitor Diniz Pagani Vieira Ribeiro dismantles that barrier**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/sergio_freitas" "Sergio Antônio Andrade de Freitas" >}}.

The work delivers two complementary pieces: a structured guide discussing how to introduce gamification in Moodle activities and courses, and a tool that operationalizes those decisions without requiring plugin or scripting knowledge. The duo deliberately chose the teacher as target user — a choice that shapes the design: language, examples, granularity, and what remains invisible to the user are all calibrated for that persona.

By pairing {{< link-interno "/areas/gamification/" "gamification" >}} with {{< link-interno "/areas/active_learning/" "active learning" >}}, the thesis narrows a classic gap: between what research knows how to do and what actually reaches the classroom.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/43543).
