Brasília, September 28, 2022

Young Brazilians reach adulthood with little fluency in personal finance — and the cost of that deficit shows up in credit card interest, poorly planned debt, and career choices made under financial pressure. **This structural problem is what the undergraduate thesis defended in September 2022 by Rafael Santos Teodosio responds to with a technology**, advised by Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/sergio_freitas" "Sergio Antônio Andrade de Freitas" >}}.

The $tudy system emerges from a twofold base: literature review of gamification frameworks — with special attention to Octalysis — and a canonical software engineering build, with requirements engineering and implementation. Choosing gamification is not decorative: learning about finance requires repetition, decision-making in consequential scenarios, and fast feedback — three things well-designed games handle particularly well.

By pairing {{< link-interno "/areas/gamification/" "gamification" >}} with {{< link-interno "/areas/active_learning/" "financial education" >}} — a rising agenda in schools — the thesis contributes to a still-incipient EdTech market focused on this theme. It is a work that bets on small daily gestures as a way to reduce an educational debt that runs across generations.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/34017).
