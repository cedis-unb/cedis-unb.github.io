Brasília, December 15, 2023

When the pandemic forced universities to run distance learning full-time, pedagogical decisions were made under emergency. Years later, the questions remain: what worked? How do the students themselves rate the experience? **This qualified hindsight is what the undergraduate thesis defended in December 2023 by Júlia Farias Sousa puts into work**, advised by Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/george_marsicano" "George Marsicano Corrêa" >}}.

The work takes the FGA/UnB Software Engineering course as a specific slice and listens to students with method — not as opinion polling, but as systematic investigation. The material collected reveals nuance: where distance learning expanded possibilities, where it shrank connection, what remained as technical legacy, and what has yet to be processed as institutional learning.

The work contributes to {{< link-interno "/areas/active_learning/" "active learning" >}} and to the contemporary discussion of hybrid methodologies — increasingly present in institutions that must respond to varied demands from increasingly diverse student profiles.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/40073).
