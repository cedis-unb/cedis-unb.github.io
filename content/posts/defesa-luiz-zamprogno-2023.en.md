Brasília, December 20, 2023

Writing a scientific article demands a kind of attention a generic chatbot cannot sustain: it requires rigor, structuring, and revision discipline. **This is the support band the undergraduate thesis defended in December 2023 by Luiz Henrique Fernandes Zamprogno and Victor Eduardo Araújo Ribeiro tries to occupy, introducing Latte** — a gamified chatbot developed under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/sergio_freitas" "Sergio Antônio Andrade de Freitas" >}}.

The system is built on the Rasa platform and integrates natural language processing resources with gamification elements crafted from the Octalysis Framework. The methodological choice is meaningful: instead of treating the chatbot as pure conversational resource, Latte relies on GCMP — Gamified Chatbot Management Process — to orchestrate the interaction flow, keeping the author engaged through stages commonly abandoned in academic writing.

By tying {{< link-interno "/areas/ai_pln/" "AI and NLP" >}} to {{< link-interno "/areas/gamification/" "gamification" >}}, the thesis enters a debate that gained weight from 2023 onward with the popularization of LLMs: how to reduce the friction of scientific writing without outsourcing thinking? Latte’s answer is indirect and specific — instrument the journey, do not produce the text.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/43473).
