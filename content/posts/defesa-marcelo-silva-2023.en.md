Brasília, February 13, 2023

Every social survey carries an implicit question about territory — but that dimension rarely makes it into the form without losing quality. **The undergraduate thesis defended in February 2023 by Marcelo Victor de Sousa Silva and Washington Bispo Arishita Júnior proposes a practical solution to that: FormsGeo**, an app developed under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/fabiana_mendes" "Fabiana Freitas Mendes" >}}.

The core idea is to integrate geolocation directly into the survey, allowing answers to be associated with coordinates with respondents’ consent. Subsequent reading gains layers: where the patterns are, which areas concentrate a given response, how the map changes over time. It is {{< link-interno "/areas/software_requirements/" "requirements engineering" >}} applied to a domain in which territorial granularity was usually sacrificed for the sake of collection simplicity.

Under a {{< link-interno "/areas/transformation/" "digital transformation" >}} lens, the work helps enable lower-cost surveys in community and academic settings — reducing dependence on proprietary platforms that absorb both the data and its interpretation.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/39194).
