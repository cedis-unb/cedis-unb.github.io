Brasília, December 22, 2023

A Community-Supported Agriculture group works because a small set of people decides to collectively sustain a rural producer. When several CSAs need to articulate — to buy together, to negotiate as a block, to share delivery infrastructure — the original software starts to show its limits. **These are the limits Abner Filipe Cunha Ribeiro and Rafael Leão Teixeira de Magalhães raised in the undergraduate thesis defended in December 2023**, advised by Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/andre_lanna" "André Luiz Peron Martins Lanna" >}}.

The work adds to AgroMart a CSA-association module in the cloud, responding to a need that only surfaced once the platform was adopted by real communities. It is a fair metaphor for the software’s own path: it starts as an MVP for a simple use case and evolves, cycle by cycle, as the user base discovers its own organizational needs.

By pairing {{< link-interno "/areas/software_architecture/" "software architecture" >}} with concerns of {{< link-interno "/areas/transformation/" "digital transformation" >}} in cooperative economics, the thesis contributes to turning AgroMart from a local app into a network platform — a shift that changes the reach of the project’s social impact.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/39809).
