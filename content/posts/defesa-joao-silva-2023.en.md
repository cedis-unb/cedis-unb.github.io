Brasília, February 14, 2023

An active-methodology course generates a lot of data — not always organized. Participation records, partial grades, group deliveries, pre-class reading activities: each piece produces signal, and they are almost never looked at together. **The undergraduate thesis defended in February 2023 by João Vitor de Moura Rosa Silva builds a system to turn that noise into agenda**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/sergio_freitas" "Sergio Antônio Andrade de Freitas" >}}.

SAMI — System for Analyzing Indicator Metrics — is the piece running through the thesis. Its promise is to make visible the indicators that the daily operation of active-methodology courses naturally produces and that tend to get lost for lack of instrument. The work does not aim to reinvent educational metrics: it proposes a path so that those already discussed in the literature gain practical support in a real classroom setting.

Operating at the crossing of {{< link-interno "/areas/learning_analytics/" "Learning Analytics" >}} and {{< link-interno "/areas/gamification/" "gamification" >}}, SAMI joins a set of works accumulating at CEDIS over recent years — evidence of a consistent research line on how to instrument teaching without bureaucratizing it.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/35949).
