Brasília, December 8, 2017

If motivation is low in the classroom, that deficit tends to hit earlier among children — and harder in math. **The undergraduate thesis defended in December 2017 by Leandro Alves Ferreira Santos starts from that diagnosis and responds with a gamified software aimed at math teaching for early school years** — advised by Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/sergio_freitas" "Sergio Antônio Andrade de Freitas" >}}.

The work touches two classic problems at once. The first is pedagogical: students and teachers face vague objectives and dwindling engagement. The second is supply: despite the abundance of educational technology, children receive little attention beyond drill-and-practice apps. By betting on {{< link-interno "/areas/gamification/" "gamification" >}}, the author targets mechanics capable of sustaining interest — rewards, progression, missions — without forgetting that content is the centerpiece, not the scenery.

The research connects with {{< link-interno "/areas/active_learning/" "active learning" >}} as the interaction with the software invites children to experiment and self-correct, rather than merely responding to closed questions. It is a concrete contribution to an age group where today’s pedagogical decisions shape long-term relationships with mathematical thinking.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/19791).
