Brasília, February 14, 2023

If each community has to run its own AgroMart copy, deployment becomes the bottleneck. **This is the bottleneck André Aben-Athar de Freitas and Pedro Vitor de Salles Cella went after in the undergraduate thesis defended in February 2023**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/andre_lanna" "André Luiz Peron Martins Lanna" >}}.

The work automates individualized platform deployment — each CSA stands up its own tailored instance with minimal manual intervention — cutting the human cost of expansion and preventing adoption from stalling for lack of technical labor. It is the difference between a platform that "exists" and one that "scales" in the sense that matters: by community will, not by the presence of original authors.

The thesis speaks to {{< link-interno "/areas/green_software/" "Green Software" >}} — through its commitment to economical, sustainable solutions — and to {{< link-interno "/areas/transformation/" "digital transformation" >}} of family farming. It is another chapter in the AgroMart saga, a project that crosses cohorts while preserving identity at every new iteration.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/35945).
