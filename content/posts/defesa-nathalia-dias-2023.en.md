Brasília, March 4, 2023

There is a question that runs through consulting engagements and retrospective meetings: if the process was carefully written, approved and rolled out, why does it stay unused? **This silent paradox is the object of the undergraduate thesis defended in March 2023 by Nathalia Lorena Cardoso Dias**, advised by Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/fabiana_mendes" "Fabiana Freitas Mendes" >}}.

The work is a systematic literature review — a format that lets one enter the question without becoming a hostage to anecdotes from a single case. The author consolidates scattered evidence about factors that explain the underuse of formal software improvement processes: distance between norm and daily practice, lack of effective sponsorship, veiled resistance, cognitive cost of adoption, among others. The result is a panorama that helps anyone about to launch yet another process to first look at what has already been launched — and did not stick.

By tying {{< link-interno "/areas/software_quality/" "software quality" >}} to an organizational lens, the thesis gives visibility to a theme almost always reduced to training. It shows that the distance between defined process and practiced process is built from small cumulative decisions — and that facing it requires more than paperwork.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/38582).
