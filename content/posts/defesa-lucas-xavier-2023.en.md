Brasília, July 25, 2023

Every software team has heard about Brazil’s General Data Protection Law. Few can answer, without hesitating, what exactly the norm demands from the code shipping tomorrow. **This gap between legal discourse and development practice is what the undergraduate thesis defended in July 2023 by Lucas Lopes Xavier seeks to reduce**, advised by Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/fabiana_mendes" "Fabiana Freitas Mendes" >}}.

The work takes seriously two texts that usually live in different cabinets: the LGPD and ISO 29100. The former sets the regulatory frame; the latter, the technical vocabulary. In the thesis, that pair becomes a privacy-requirements catalog that can go straight into an application’s backlog — with verification criteria, decision points, and clear trade-offs.

By speaking to {{< link-interno "/areas/software_quality/" "software quality" >}}, the thesis delivers the pragmatic step that vague synonyms such as “privacy by design” tend to avoid: turning principles into verifiable artifacts. It is a useful work both for teams already operating in compliance and for those unsure of where to start.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/39153).
