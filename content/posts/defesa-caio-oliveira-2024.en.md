Brasília, September 12, 2024

Individual evaluation in Software Engineering courses is a double task: care for pedagogical fairness and respond to scale. **The undergraduate thesis defended in September 2024 by Caio César Oliveira proposes AI as a support to the instructor in that job**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/george_marsicano" "George Marsicano Corrêa" >}}.

The work departs from a real scenario: the Software Requirements and Software Development Methods courses at FCTE/UnB, whose assessments run through Microsoft Forms. Around that funnel, the author designs an intelligent assistant that helps the instructor organize responses, identify patterns, flag labels worth reviewing, and — when applicable — propose grading priorities.

By tying {{< link-interno "/areas/ai_pln/" "AI" >}} to concrete {{< link-interno "/areas/active_learning/" "pedagogical practice" >}}, the thesis enters a discussion the academic community cannot postpone: the role of intelligent assistants in evaluation, without letting judgment slip inside a black box. It is a pilot work — and useful precisely for that — sketching a solution that can be tested, adjusted, and critiqued in use.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/40076).
