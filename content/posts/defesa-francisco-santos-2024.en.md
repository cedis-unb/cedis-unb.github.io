Brasília, March 11, 2024

Usability is one of those characteristics whose evolution is made of small, cumulative steps, hard to justify in isolation. **The undergraduate thesis defended in March 2024 by Francisco Heronildo Sousa Santos and João Vitor Ferreira Alves organizes those small steps into a structured cycle**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/fabiana_mendes" "Fabiana Freitas Mendes" >}}.

The work grounds its methodological choice in usability mechanisms already consolidated in HCI literature — not in loose heuristics — and applies them to a specific web application in an evaluate-and-improve regime. The duo observes user behavior, identifies friction points, proposes interventions, and measures before-and-after with objective criteria.

This approach brings {{< link-interno "/areas/software_quality/" "software quality" >}} into the daily life of a real product, avoiding the trap of discussing usability in the abstract. The result is not only an illustrated case but a replicable process for other applications — usually far more valuable than any one-off redesign.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/39807).
