Brasília, September 18, 2024

Democratizing access to legal information first requires solving a paradox: those who most need to know have the least time and the least technical vocabulary to navigate dense texts. **The undergraduate thesis defended in September 2024 by Guilherme Rogelin Vial and Lucas Braun Vieira Xavier attacks that paradox with a low-cost tooling choice**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/andre_lanna" "André Luiz Peron Martins Lanna" >}}.

The work leans on a curious methodological choice: using Google Forms as the interface to build legal flows. The decision shifts the technical task to a platform familiar to lawyers and instructors — who can model decision paths without depending on a programmer. The result is an instrument in which legal professionals design the journey of those consulting the service, turning fragmented knowledge into a navigable script.

By placing {{< link-interno "/areas/software_architecture/" "software architecture" >}} under the lens of legal education, the thesis signals a path to reduce content maintenance costs in a domain that changes every day — and that demands public, free, reusable access.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/40136).
