Brasília, December 15, 2020

Traditional testing executes a script; exploratory testing executes a hypothesis. **This distinction — more didactic than usually acknowledged — is what the undergraduate thesis defended by Iolane Caroline Alves de Andrade in December 2020 puts into practice**, advised by Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/ricardo_ajax" "Ricardo Ajax Dias Kosloski" >}}.

The work carries out a functional evaluation of a software product by combining both approaches. On the scripted side, tests verify that expected outputs materialize. On the exploratory side, testers freely probe the system searching for unexpected behavior — with the side effect of producing learning about the product itself. This dual observation gives body to the thesis’s practical contribution: a reference for teams deciding where each type of testing should sit in the development cycle.

The work belongs to {{< link-interno "/areas/verival/" "verification, validation and testing" >}} and reinforces the argument that {{< link-interno "/areas/software_quality/" "software quality" >}} cannot rest on a single approach — it depends on the disciplined, combined use of complementary techniques.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/30378).
