Brasília, July 24, 2023

There is a structural difference between gamifying a course on paper and sustaining it in day-to-day operation — when fifty students need real-time feedback, points must be counted without error margins, and the instructor cannot turn into a spreadsheet-keeper. **The undergraduate thesis defended by Ítalo Alves Guimarães in July 2023 delivers the instrument that difference demands**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/sergio_freitas" "Sergio Antônio Andrade de Freitas" >}}.

The work implements an application to support the gamified Human-Computer Interaction course offered at FCTE/UnB. It is not a generic tool: it is an instrument born of use, shaped by prior rounds of gamification applied to that specific setting. This marriage between {{< link-interno "/areas/gamification/" "gamification" >}} and {{< link-interno "/areas/active_learning/" "active learning" >}} is what produces relevance — because once daily friction is removed, the instructor is freed for what technique cannot replace: mediating learning.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/36833).
