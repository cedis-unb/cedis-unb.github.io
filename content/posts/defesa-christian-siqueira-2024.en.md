Brasília, September 17, 2024

The last mile of any software project is the cheapest to describe and the most expensive to travel. **This is the mile Christian Fleury Alencar Siqueira and Thiago Siqueira Gomes crossed in the undergraduate thesis defended in September 2024**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/andre_lanna" "André Luiz Peron Martins Lanna" >}}.

The work gathers modules developed across several AgroMart generations, resolves friction points between them, and brings the platform to the state required for publication — the moment when software stops being an academic project and turns into a product capable of sustaining real use. The duo handles integration, deployment adjustments, and final testing, delivering an AgroMart that, for the first time, reaches its intended audience without passing through a technical intermediary.

By tying {{< link-interno "/areas/software_architecture/" "software architecture" >}} with {{< link-interno "/areas/transformation/" "digital transformation" >}} focused on social impact, the thesis fulfills an old promise of the project: bringing to the small farmer a tool that responds to their problems, not to those of its builders.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/40098).
