Brasília, December 2, 2025

Footvolley grew from Rio’s beach boardwalk into dozens of regional circuits across Brazil — and, like any rising sport, begins to demand from technique what intuition can no longer sustain alone. **The undergraduate thesis defended in December 2025 by Felipe Direito Corrieri de Macedo enters through that door with a concrete proposal: FTV Scout**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/ricardo_ajax" "Ricardo Ajax Dias Kosloski" >}}.

The system offers notation and performance analysis tailored to the sport — not as a generic adaptation of volleyball or football tools, but with attention to the rules and dynamics specific to footvolley. The work takes seriously the instrumentation of a sport that still operates without consolidated statistical infrastructure and, in doing so, shows that the value of {{< link-interno "/areas/transformation/" "digital transformation" >}} rarely sits in the loudest agendas — it often sits inside specific communities willing to experiment with technique.

The result is a useful tool for coaches, athletes, and circuit organizers — and an example of how an undergraduate thesis finds a real problem when it listens to the communities that play.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/43576).
