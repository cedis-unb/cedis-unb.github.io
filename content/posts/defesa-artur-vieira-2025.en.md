Brasília, February 18, 2025

Notebooks, PDFs, class recordings, whiteboard screenshots, WhatsApp links: the material that sustains today’s engineering student’s learning is scattered across formats and locations. **The undergraduate thesis defended in February 2025 by Artur de Sousa Vieira and Luíza Esteves dos Santos attacks this chaos with a specific piece: OMA — Academic Multimedia Organizer**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/ricardo_ajax" "Ricardo Ajax Dias Kosloski" >}}.

The application is designed for Engineering students at Campus Gama and solves a problem as practical as it is invisible: gathering class notes in one place, adding media by type, tying each piece to context (course, topic, week). Treating the problem as a real project case gives the thesis the useful character of a complete exercise — from needs elicitation to deliverable product.

By pairing {{< link-interno "/areas/active_learning/" "active learning" >}} with {{< link-interno "/areas/software_architecture/" "software architecture" >}}, OMA offers a small but useful piece of infrastructure for a generation of students whose academic life must be digital without becoming clutter.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/43564).
