Brasília, September 26, 2022

Higher education dropout is one of the costliest indicators for universities and for students themselves — and one of the least observed with enough granularity for coordinated action. **This bottleneck is what Amanda Emilly Muniz de Menezes and Letícia Karla Soares Rodrigues de Araújo tackled in their undergraduate thesis**, defended in September 2022 at FCTE/UnB under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/cristiane_ramos" "Cristiane Soares Ramos" >}} and Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/sergio_freitas" "Sergio Antônio Andrade de Freitas" >}}.

The work begins with a systematic literature review to identify which factors — academic, demographic, learning-related — are consistently associated with early student departure. On that foundation, the duo builds predictive models using machine learning algorithms and shows that predictions respond sensitively to changes in course flows: reorganizing curricula, for instance, changes dropout risk for specific cohorts.

The finding matters for {{< link-interno "/areas/learning_analytics/" "Learning Analytics" >}} and institutional policy: aligning pedagogical decisions with risk evidence enables targeted interventions — instead of generic campaigns, whose impact is never measured. It is a work that signals a clear direction for universities willing to take data seriously.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/34952).
