Brasília, July 23, 2025

Often overlooked, much of a student’s daily wear in a large university comes from the difficulty of finding basic information: where to book a lab, how to request a certificate, when the internship deadline expires. **The undergraduate thesis defended in July 2025 by Breno Henrique de Souza and Giulia Domingues de Alcantara aims at that friction with a chatbot focused on FCTE**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/george_marsicano" "George Marsicano Corrêa" >}}.

The duo builds a solution that combines AI support with a centralized knowledge base of the faculty’s administrative and pedagogical information. Choosing a chatbot as the interface is useful precisely because of the target audience: students use messaging as their default channel, and a conversational agent surfaces the relevant answer without requiring navigation through institutional directories.

By tying {{< link-interno "/areas/ai_pln/" "AI" >}} to the {{< link-interno "/areas/active_learning/" "student experience" >}}, the thesis contributes to lowering the daily cost this faculty imposes on its community — and frees in-person services for demands that actually require human attention.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/42443).
