Brasília, July 24, 2023

Many people learned how to work as a group, negotiate goals, and deal with uncertainty around an RPG table — not inside a classroom. That observation is easy to make; turning it into pedagogy is harder. **This is the challenge the undergraduate thesis defended by João Vitor Lopes de Farias in July 2023 takes on**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/sergio_freitas" "Sergio Antônio Andrade de Freitas" >}}.

The work — named Jornada — treats the RPG not as decoration, but as the backbone of a learning process. Narrative, roles, chained challenges, and immediate feedback stop being playful accessories and become pedagogical instruments. The operational question is: how to tie specific learning goals to campaign arcs while preserving the sense of freedom that makes RPGs work?

By moving between {{< link-interno "/areas/gamification/" "gamification" >}} and {{< link-interno "/areas/active_learning/" "active learning" >}}, the thesis opens a rarely explored path: instead of gamifying in layers, adopt an entire game as the form. It is a work that interests both teachers willing to experiment and instructional designers looking for solid reference to underpin the bet.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/36942).
