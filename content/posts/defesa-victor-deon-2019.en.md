Brasília, July 12, 2019

Teamwork, data analysis, and real-world problem solving — three competencies the market demands and that traditional classrooms deliver only halfway. **Team-Based Learning (TBL) has been pointed to as a path across that gap, and it is around it that Victor Hugo Arnaud Deon built his undergraduate thesis**, defended in July 2019 at FCTE/UnB under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/cristiane_ramos" "Cristiane Soares Ramos" >}}.

The work introduces PGTBL — Team-Based Learning Management Platform —, an open-source piece of software that operationalizes the methodology’s steps: team formation, individual and collective assessments, group discussion, and feedback. The choice of open source is deliberate and matters: public and private higher education institutions can adopt, adapt, and evolve the tool without the lock-in that plagues proprietary platforms.

By connecting {{< link-interno "/areas/soft_skills/" "teamwork" >}} with {{< link-interno "/areas/active_learning/" "active learning methodologies" >}}, the thesis offers a piece of infrastructure for instructors willing to experiment with TBL without having to reinvent the whole activity-management scaffolding — often the primary reason people give up when trying to move away from lecture-based teaching.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/23047).
