Brasília, May 28, 2021

Active methodologies gain courses — and gain classroom questions. How to tell whether groups are actually learning? Where should the material be redosed? Which student is at risk of falling behind? **The undergraduate thesis defended in May 2021 by Allan Jefrey Pereira Nobre steps in and proposes to turn Team-Based Learning activity data into pedagogical decisions** — advised by Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/cristiane_ramos" "Cristiane Soares Ramos" >}}.

The theoretical base is twofold: {{< link-interno "/areas/learning_analytics/" "Learning Analytics" >}} — collecting and interpreting learning data to improve the process — and Educational Data Mining, which brings statistical and machine-learning tools to uncover patterns in educational data. By combining the two lenses, the author discusses how a teacher can move from subjective perception (“I think they got it”) to evidence-based observation.

The work does not treat TBL as a black box — it treats it as an instrumented system, where well-chosen collection points feed everyday decisions: which teams to call for additional discussion, where to adjust the pre-reading, when to change the difficulty level of a question.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://www.bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/33678).
