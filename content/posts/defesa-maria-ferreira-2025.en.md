Brasília, July 17, 2025

Selecting viable embryos is one of the most critical steps of in vitro fertilization — and one where every invasive intervention carries a biological cost. **The duo Maria Eduarda dos Santos Abritta Ferreira and Sabrina Caldas Berno took on this challenge in the undergraduate thesis defended in July 2025**, under the advising of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/george_marsicano" "George Marsicano Corrêa" >}}.

The work investigates whether morphokinetic data — the evolution of cell divisions captured by time-lapse systems — carry enough signal to predict euploidy without requiring biopsy. The question is at once clinical and computational: IVF needs evidence-supported decisions, and the available evidence lives in temporal images that only systematic analysis can turn into prediction.

By applying {{< link-interno "/areas/ai_pln/" "machine learning" >}} to a specific biomedical problem, the thesis contributes to a frontier where clinicians and computer scientists still develop shared vocabulary. It is a work of interest to reproductive-assistance labs in the country — and to researchers willing to cross disciplines in order to treat decisions of high human impact with more rigor.

The full text — written in Portuguese — is available at the [UnB Undergraduate Theses Digital Library](https://bdm.unb.br/handle/10483/42616).
