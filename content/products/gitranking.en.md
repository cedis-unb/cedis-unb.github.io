## Project Description

**Git Ranking** is a gamified tool to support Scrum, developed as a **GitHub App** for managing and motivating agile teams. The product was developed by **Ana Beatriz Norberto da Silva** and **Beatriz Vieira Nascimento** as a bachelor's thesis under the supervision of Prof. {{< link-interno "/people/sergio_freitas" "Sergio Freitas" >}}, with academic support from the **Center for Studies, Development, and Innovation in Software (CEDIS)**.

The solution integrates elements of the **Octalysis** framework to balance teams' intrinsic and extrinsic motivators, consuming **GitHub Webhooks** processed by a **Node.js** backend and presented through a **React/Next.js** interface.

## Project Objectives

The main objectives of the project are:

- to help software development teams maintain continuous engagement and the visibility of individual progress within agile methodologies;
- to provide a metrics dashboard and an achievements profile based on developers' actual actions in the repositories;
- to apply gamification techniques selected from the teams' motivational profile, identified with support from the Intrinsic Motivation Inventory (IMI) and the Octalysis Core Drives; and
- to serve as an experimental basis for teaching, research, and outreach initiatives supported by CEDIS in gamification and software engineering.

## Resources and Features

Among the features offered by **Git Ranking** are integrated login via GitHub, organization and repository selection, a Sprint tracking and progress panel (Overview), and a metrics dashboard with detailed visual feedback on the team's technical progress.

The application also offers a developer profile with level, badges, and social feedback received, a team members list with interpersonal interactions, voting for "Sprint Hero," peer-to-peer recognition, and visual feedback such as confetti effects when milestones are reached.
